Hydraulic Fracturing: Fundamentals and Advancements, With an author team of 26 subject-matter experts representing a diversity of talent, background, and experience, Hydraulic Fracturing: Fundamentals and Advancements delivers a comprehensive discussion on the principles of hydraulic fracturing while also including the latest processes that have prompted the explosive growth in stimulating horizontal wells in shale and tight oil and gas reservoirs.

Hydraulic fracturing is a technique to stimulate the productivity of a well. Hydraulic fracturing belongs to the newer techniques in petroleum sciences, not older than approximately 50 years. A hydraulic fracture is a superimposed structure that remains undisturbed outside the fracture. Thus, the effective permeability of a reservoir remains unchanged by this process. The increased productivity results from the increased wellbore radius, because in the course of hydraulic fracturing, a large contact surface between the well and the reservoir is created. Complex chemical formulations are used hydraulic fracturing fluids.

Nodal Analysis of Oil and Gas Production Systems, Nodal Analysis of Oil and Gas Production Systems provides a modern view on the use of nodal analysis techniques to optimize the production from oil and gas wells. It covers multiphase flow through flow lines, wells, chokes and the near-well reservoir. It teaches the development of mathematical models for those system elements, and how to implement these in computer code. It explains the underlying physics, starting from first principles whenever possible, and reverting to empirical correlations whenever necessary. It covers the essential concepts in nodal analysis of a well system and aims at providing the readers with enough knowledge to use commercial nodal analysis software for more complex systems, explore the literature in this field, and expand their knowledge.

Nodal analysis can be a model of a gas or an oil well. It can simulate a single well’s performance including such components with multiphase flow as the flowline, a surface or downhole choke, tubing performance, completion effects such as perforations, and inflow performance (vertical flow and approximations to horizontal well). If simulated approximately well, the user can then see what impacts in the variations of in tubing size, choke size, surface pressure, and inflow can have on the performance of the well.

Enhanced Oil Recovery EOR, An invaluable tool for petroleum engineering students, Enhanced Oil Recovery also serves as an important resource for those practicing oil recovery in the field or engaged in the design and operation of commercial projects involving enhanced-or improved-oil-recovery processes. A prior understanding of basic petrophysics, fluid properties, and material balance is recommended.

Did you know that only 30 to 40% of the oil contained in reservoirs is extracted? For many years now we've been working on implementing enhanced oil recovery, or EOR, methods to boost our fields’ potential and extend their life. The stakes are huge. Increasing the amount of oil recovered by just one percentage point would work out to an additional 18 months of global consumption.

Diseño y Evaluación del Proceso de Obtención de Biolubricante a partir Biomasa de Segunda Generación, En la actualidad la demanda de combustibles y lubricantes a nivel Bolivia tiende a incrementarse linealmente, así también las importaciones de aditivos, ga-solina, diesel, lubricantes, grasas y otros derivados; generando también aumentos drásticos en la subvención y la salida de divisas, impulsando a que la balanza co-mercial tienda a un déficit. Por otra parte, la disminución de la producción de pe-tróleo y gas de los megacampos impelan la óptica hacia nuevas fuentes de energía, tales como los biocombustibles, biolubricantes, fotovoltaicas, eólicas, procesos de electrolisis y otros.

Por los motivos expuestos, este proyecto se enfocó en la evaluación tecnoló-gica para la producción de lubricantes a partir de biomasa de segunda generación, donde la materia prima más prometedora resulto ser los aceites vegetales no co-mestibles ya que no llegan a competir con el sector alimentario. También, se de-terminó que la producción de lubricantes a 2030 llega a ser mínima, teniendo este proyecto la obligación de abarcar la totalidad de la oferta proyectada para mercado interno. También, se evaluó económicamente el monto de inversión para la presente propuesta, minimizando costo gracias a aplicación de la integración a refinerías y la implementación de procesos combinados para reducir la dependencia del uso del gas natural; resultando un monto de inversión de 32 millones de dólares america-nos para una planta de producción de 1,44 millones de barriles por año de biodiesel, el cual será empleado como materia prima para el proceso de segunda transesterifi-cación produciendo 400 mil barriles por año de lubricante base.
